## What is DNA parentage verification?

Some registries require DNA parentage testing to verify that an animal's recorded sire and dam are its biological parents. This testing is done by an approved genetics laboratory.

When a registrar requests a DNA test, you will need to:
1. Contact an approved laboratory (your registry may specify which labs are accepted)
2. Submit a sample (blood, hair, or swab — the lab will advise)
3. Receive a results PDF from the lab
4. Upload the PDF to your registration application in CapraGene

## Understanding DNA trust tiers

Every registered animal is assigned a **DNA Trust Tier** based on how much of its parentage has been verified:

| Tier | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Both sire and dam DNA verified — highest confidence |
| **Tier 2** | One parent DNA verified |
| **Tier 3** | Pedigree recorded but no DNA testing done |
| **Tier 0** | DNA mismatch detected — parentage disputed |

The tier badge is shown on the animal's registration certificate and on the public registry search page.

## Uploading a DNA document

When your registration status shows **DNA Required**:

1. Open **My Registrations**.
2. Find the registration showing **DNA Required**.
3. Click **Submit DNA Document**.
4. Enter the **laboratory name**.
5. Select and upload your **results PDF** from your device.
6. Click **Submit**.

Your application status will change to **DNA Submitted**. The registrar will review the document and either verify it or request a resubmission.

## If your DNA test fails

If the registrar cannot verify the DNA document:
- Your status will return to **DNA Required**
- You will receive a notification with any notes from the registrar
- You can upload a new document following the same steps above
