<Note>
  The Genetics features are available on the **Stud plan** only.
</Note>

## Overview

The Genetics tab is available inside each animal's profile under the **Breeding** section. It has three parts:

- **Genetic Profile** — record the genetic markers known for this animal
- **Punnett Square Predictor** — see the probability of each outcome when crossing this animal with a specific mate
- **Breeding Pair Optimiser** — rank every eligible animal in your herd by how well they match your target traits

## Recording genetic markers

1. Open an animal's profile and click the **Breeding** tab.
2. Select the **Genetics** sub-tab.
3. Click **Add Marker**.
4. Choose a **locus** from the list (e.g. Polled/Horned, Extension, Agouti, Casein variants).
5. Enter the two **alleles** for this animal (e.g. P and p for a heterozygous polled animal).
6. Optionally tick **Verified by DNA test** if lab results confirm the genotype.
7. Click **Save**.

Repeat for each known locus. You can edit or delete markers at any time.

## Using the Punnett Square Predictor

The Punnett Square shows the probability of each offspring genotype and phenotype when this animal is crossed with a selected mate.

1. In the **Genetics** sub-tab, scroll to **Punnett Square Predictor**.
2. Select a potential mate from the dropdown. Only animals in your herd with genetic markers recorded will appear.
3. The predictor shows a grid for each shared locus — for example, a 75% chance of polled offspring.
4. Results show the genotype (e.g. PP, Pp, pp) and the expected phenotype (e.g. Polled, Carrier, Horned) with probability percentages.

<Tip>
  If no animals appear in the mate dropdown, add genetic markers to other animals in your herd first.
</Tip>

## Using the Breeding Pair Optimiser

The Optimiser ranks every herd mate by how well a pairing is likely to produce your target traits.

1. Scroll to the **Breeding Pair Optimiser** section.
2. For each locus shown, use the dropdown to set your desired outcome:
   - **Any outcome** — no preference for this trait
   - **Dominant** — prefer offspring that express the dominant phenotype
   - **Recessive** — prefer offspring that express the recessive phenotype
3. Click **Find Best Mates**.
4. Results are ranked by match score (0–100%).

<Tip>
  Leaving all dropdowns on "Any outcome" ranks mates purely by how many genetic markers they share — useful for a general compatibility overview.
</Tip>

## Supported loci

| Locus | What it controls |
|---|---|
| Polled / Horned | Presence or absence of horns |
| Extension (MC1R) | Base coat colour (black vs red/brown) |
| Agouti | Pattern distribution of pigment |
| Brown (TYRP1) | Brown vs black pigment |
| Spotting | White spotting patterns |
| Casein α-S1 | Milk protein variant (A, B, C, F) |
| Casein β | Milk protein variant |
| Casein κ | Milk protein variant |

<Note>
  Casein loci are co-dominant and shown in the Genetic Profile and Punnett Square only — they are not included in the Breeding Pair Optimiser.
</Note>
